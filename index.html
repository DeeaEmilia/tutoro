Reading

Reading is an important aspect of language learning because it helps to improve vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension. By reading texts written in the target language, learners are exposed to a variety of sentence structures and vocabulary words that they may not encounter in conversation. Additionally, reading allows learners to gain a deeper understanding of the culture and context of the language they are learning. Furthermore, it is also a great way to practice and improve reading comprehension, which can be challenging when learning a new language.

What we will do together:

  • Assign reading materials at the student's level, such as short stories, articles, or news articles, that are interesting and relevant to them.
  • Ask the student comprehension questions about the text to check their understanding.
  • Have the student summarize the text or retell it in their own words.
  • Encourage the student to look up words they don't know and add them to their vocabulary list.
  • Have the student read aloud to practice pronunciation and intonation

Writing

Writing is a crucial element of language learning because it helps learners to practice their grammar and vocabulary in a more formal setting. Writing essays, emails, or even journal entries in the target language allows learners to focus on grammar rules and sentence structure. Additionally, writing can help learners to improve their spelling and punctuation, as well as their ability to express themselves clearly and effectively in the language they are learning.

What we will do together:

  • Assign writing tasks such as composing emails, writing a short story or an essay, or keeping a journal in the target language.
  • Review the student's writing and provide feedback on grammar, vocabulary, and sentence structure.
  • Encourage the student to use online resources such as grammar checkers or writing guides to improve their writing skills.
×

Listening

Listening is an essential skill for language learners because it helps them to understand spoken language in real-world situations. By listening to native speakers, learners can improve their comprehension and pronunciation. Additionally, listening to music, podcasts, and audio books in the target language can help learners to become more familiar with the rhythm and intonation of the language.

What we will do together:

  • Play audio recordings or videos in the target language, such as podcasts, music, or news broadcasts.
  • Ask the student comprehension questions about what they heard and have them retell the main points.
  • Encourage the student to listen to native speakers and notice the intonation, rhythm and stress, and also to use listening exercises and dictations.
×

Conversation

Conversation is a key element of language learning because it allows learners to practice their speaking skills in a real-world setting. By engaging in conversation with native speakers, learners can improve their pronunciation, fluency, and confidence in the language they are learning. Additionally, conversation allows learners to learn idiomatic expressions and colloquial language that is not typically taught in a classroom setting.

What we will do together:

  • Encourage the student to engage in conversations with native speakers, either in person or through online language exchange platforms.
  • Provide the student with conversation prompts and questions to help them practice speaking.
  • Correct the student's mistakes in grammar, vocabulary and pronunciation during our conversation sessions.
  • Provide the student with opportunities to practice idiomatic expressions and colloquial language
×

Grammar

Grammar is an important element of language learning because it helps learners to understand how sentences are constructed and how words relate to each other. By studying grammar, learners can improve their ability to form correct sentences, understand the meaning of text, and communicate effectively. Additionally, understanding grammar rules can help learners to avoid common mistakes and sound more like native speakers.

What we will do together:

  • Provide the student with grammar exercises, such as fill-in-the-blank, multiple-choice, or matching exercises.
  • Review the student's work and provide feedback on grammar and sentence structure.
  • Encourage the student to use online resources such as grammar guides to improve their understanding of grammar rules.
×

Vocabulary

Vocabulary is a fundamental element of language learning because it enables learners to understand and express themselves in the target language. By studying vocabulary, learners can improve their reading comprehension, writing skills, and speaking fluency. Additionally, building a strong vocabulary allows learners to communicate more effectively and understand more complex texts. Furthermore, learning vocabulary in context, using images and mnemonics can help learners to retain words more effectively.

What we will do together:

  • Provide the student with a list of vocabulary words that are relevant and interesting to them.
  • Encourage the student to use flashcards, mnemonics, and other memory aids to help them learn new words.
  • Test the student on their vocabulary regularly to track their progress.
  • Encourage the student to use the new words in context, such as in writing or conversation.
